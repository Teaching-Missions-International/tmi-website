About Us

Dedicated Educators of Biblical Reformed Theology

Teaching  Missions International (TMI) is a 501(c)(3) (non-profit) organization dedicated to  providing, to those church leaders who cannot afford, whether for lack  of time or money, sound Biblical Reformed theological training to help them grow in God's grace and better lead their churches in their respective  nations.
 

We are a cadre of mostly Presbyterian Church in America (PCA) congregants, including current and retired pastors, professors and other leaders, and are involved with a number of partners, including  Mission to the World (MTW), Reformed Evangelistic Fellowship (REF), the International Presbyterian Church and Reformation Hope. We are convicted by  Hosea 4:6, "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge," and we have  dedicated our gifts to the Lord in closing this gap.

Where We've Instructed

 TMI has experience teaching theology in Haiti, Ukraine, India, Bulgaria, Grenada, Mexico, Colombia, Cuba, Hispanic communities in Houston, TX and Providence, RI and soon, Bolivia, Antigua, Dominica, St. Martin, Jamaica, St.Kitts and St. Vincent and the Grenadines.  

What We Teach

We teach Biblical Reformed theology in both structured degree  programs and one-off courses (e.g. biblical marriage). With our partner, Reformation Hope, we  have set up a complete Bible College curriculum (L’Académie Théologique de la Réforme, fully accredited by  the federal government of Haiti) that we are teaching to native Haitian  pastors, having graduated our first Bachelor of Divinity class. We are  also teaching in a similar curriculum in Latin America and various curricula, including MINTS and custom courses, in other countries. Additionally, we rolled out a Master of Arts in Theological Studies  (MATS) in Haiti in September '17, a PhD program in October of 2018, and a DMin program in February '20. Any of our courses can be tailored to fit conferences, short seminars, church leaders, or simply congregants who want/need solid reformed training in God's  Word.
